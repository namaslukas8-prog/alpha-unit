Most Common Threat: Phishing

Criminals use emails, texts, and fake websites to steal your passwords and money. They're successful because they look legitimate. Learn to spot the red flags.

Learn about phishing

Most Important Protection: MFA

Multi-factor authentication (2FA) blocks 99% of account takeovers. Even if your password is stolen, MFA prevents attackers from accessing your account. Enable it on email, banking, and social media.

Learn about MFA

Most Critical Moment: First 24 Hours

If you suspect you've been hacked, the first 24 hours are critical. Change passwords, enable MFA, alert your bank. Speed prevents further damage.

Get emergency help

The Golden Rule: If a message creates urgency ("act now", "limited time", "your account will be closed"), asks for login/password/payment details, or comes from an unexpected sender slow down and verify directly with the organization before responding.
